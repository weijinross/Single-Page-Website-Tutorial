step one
WHAT'S YOUR BUDGET?

although univeristy tuition in germany is free, there are still many costs to consider. a part of your income will have to go to taxes,insurance and pension payments. you must also pay rent,internet, electricity and a few other things. if you buy a car, you must think about vehicle tax, car insurance and maintenance. N26 is a good bank if you just moved to germany. they let you open an account without an anmeldebestätigung, they speak english, and they have very low fees.

step two
PREPARE FOR YOUR NEW CAREER

if you work in tech and you don't speak german, finding a job in berlin will still be very easy. english is the main language of many offices in Berlin, especially in tech companies. there is a huge demand for software developers and IT workers in berlin. you will need a visa to work here, obtaining one can take 4-12 weeks.

step three
WHICH DISTRICT SUITS YOU BEST?

Kreuzberg

the tech centre, with start-ups setting up shop in the neighbourhood. it’s so tech-friendly that many of the bars in the area even accept bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies as payment.

Rixdorf

the cobblestone streets and former farm buildings are a novelty within berlin. with some much-loved traditional restaurants and cafés in the vicinity.

Neukölln

tons of bars, cafés, independent galleries and vintage stores. you’ll find an array of cool candle-lit bars and independent shops, as well as tattoo studios, creative spaces and arthouse cinemas.

step four
FIND A PLACE TO LIVE

it's really hard to find an apartment in berlin. there is a lot of competition. after you find an apartment, you must register your address. when you register your address, you get a tax ID, and an anmeldebestätigung. your employer needs your tax ID to calculate your taxes correctly. you should also join a tenant's association (mieterverein). it costs around 10€ per month. they can review your lease and tell you if you pay too much rent. if you have problems with your landlord, they can give you legal assistance.

step five
GATHER THE NECESSARY DOCUMENTS

health insurance is mandatory in germany. an independent health insurance broker can help you choose the right health insurance. their help is free. you can also use websites like tarifcheck to compare health insurance options, but talking to a broker is a better idea. the cheapest option is not always the best option for you.

step seven
PICK A UNIVERSITY

Humboldt-Universität zu Berlin

regarded as the world's preeminent university for the natural sciences during the 19th and early 20th century, as the university is linked to major breakthroughs in physics and other sciences by its professors, such as Albert Einstein.

Universität der Künste Berlin

one of the biggest and most diversified universities of the arts worldwide. it has four colleges specialising in fine arts, architecture, media and design, music and the performing arts with around 3,500 students.

Technische Universität Berlin

notable for having been the first to offer a degree in industrial engineering and management (wirtschaftsingenieurwesen). the university designed the degree in response to requests by industrialists for graduates with the technical and management training to run a company.

step eight
PREPARE FOR A CRAZY NIGHTLIFE

unlike other german cities, berlin has no official closing time. the berlin nightlife really does spoil for choice, especially in the techno department where the city is recognised as a leading light around the world. as well as the standard rock, alternative and techno clubs you'll also find tons of local breweries and beer gardens!

step nine
PLAN SOME FUN ACTIVITIES

berlin is known for its numerous cultural institutions, many of which enjoy international reputation. the diversity and vivacity of the metropolis led to a trendsetting atmosphere. an innovative music, dance and art scene has developed in the 21st century. berlin contains three world heritage sites: museum island; the palaces and parks of potsdam and berlin; and the berlin modernism housing estates.
